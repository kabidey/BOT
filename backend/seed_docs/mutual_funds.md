# Mutual Funds

## Overview
Mutual Funds are pooled investment vehicles regulated by SEBI under the SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996. An asset management company (AMC) collects capital from numerous investors and deploys it across a diversified portfolio in accordance with a stated investment objective. **Mackertich ONE**, the wealth-management vertical of SMIFS Ltd, offers mutual fund advisory and execution across all major AMC platforms with a research-first selection framework.

## Categories
SEBI's October 2017 categorisation circular standardised mutual funds into the following primary buckets:

### Equity Funds
- **Large Cap**: Minimum 80% in top-100 companies by market capitalisation.
- **Mid Cap**: Minimum 65% in companies ranked 101–250.
- **Small Cap**: Minimum 65% in companies ranked beyond 250.
- **Multi Cap**: Minimum 75% in equities with at least 25% each in large, mid, and small.
- **Flexi Cap**: Minimum 65% in equities; no fixed allocation across cap segments.
- **ELSS**: Equity-Linked Savings Scheme with a three-year lock-in; eligible for Section 80C deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh per FY.

### Debt Funds
- **Liquid**: Investments in money-market instruments with residual maturity up to 91 days.
- **Ultra Short Duration**: Macaulay duration 3–6 months.
- **Short Duration**: Macaulay duration 1–3 years.
- **Corporate Bond**: Minimum 80% in highest-rated corporate bonds.
- **Gilt**: Minimum 80% in government securities.
- **Credit Risk**: Minimum 65% in below highest-rated corporate paper; higher yield, higher credit risk.

### Hybrid Funds
- **Aggressive Hybrid**: 65%–80% equity, balance debt.
- **Conservative Hybrid**: 10%–25% equity, balance debt.
- **Balanced Advantage / Dynamic Asset Allocation**: Equity-debt mix flexed based on valuation models.
- **Multi-Asset Allocation**: Minimum 10% each in at least three asset classes (equity, debt, gold).

## Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs)
A SIP allows an investor to commit a fixed amount at regular intervals (weekly, monthly, quarterly). SIPs harness rupee-cost averaging and remove timing decisions. SMIFS recommends SIPs as the default mode of equity accumulation for clients with goals over a 5+ year horizon. The minimum SIP across most schemes is ₹500 per month.

## Expense Ratios
SEBI caps expense ratios on a slab basis tied to scheme AUM. Equity schemes carry expense ratios of 0.5%–2.25% depending on AUM and direct-vs-regular plan choice. Direct plans (purchased without an intermediary) carry a lower expense ratio than regular plans by 50–100 basis points annually. Over a 20-year horizon, the cost differential can compound to 15%–20% of terminal corpus. SMIFS executes all client mutual fund transactions in Direct Plan mode while charging a transparent advisory fee.

## Taxation
- **Equity-oriented funds** (≥65% equity): STCG at 15% (≤12 months), LTCG at 10% above ₹1 lakh per FY (>12 months).
- **Debt-oriented funds** (per April 2023 amendments): All gains taxed at slab rate regardless of holding period.
- **Hybrid funds**: Tax treatment follows their predominant equity exposure as classified by the AMC.

Dividends from all categories are taxed at slab rate in the investor's hands.
