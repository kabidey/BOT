# IPO and Primary Market Services

## Overview
**Mackertich ONE**, the wealth-management vertical of SMIFS Ltd, offers a comprehensive primary market desk covering Mainboard IPOs, SME IPOs, Follow-on Public Offers (FPOs), Offer for Sale (OFS), Rights Issues, and public NCD issuances. Our objective is to help clients participate in well-priced primary opportunities while filtering out issues with weak fundamentals or aggressive valuation.

## Mainboard IPOs
Mainboard IPOs are listings on the BSE Mainboard or NSE Main Platform — typically issuers with paid-up capital above ₹10 crore and a minimum public offer of ₹10 crore. Allocation is governed by SEBI:

- **QIB**: 50% reserved (or up to 75% for book-built issues with profitability waiver).
- **NII / HNI**: 15%, further bifurcated into Small HNI (₹2L–₹10L) and Big HNI (>₹10L) buckets in a 1:2 ratio.
- **Retail**: 35% reserved, individual applications up to ₹2 lakh.

SMIFS provides issue analysis covering business model, growth track record, peer comparison on EV/EBITDA and P/E, anchor investor quality, promoter holding post-listing, and use of proceeds. We do not advise on issues where the use of proceeds is dominated by Offer for Sale by promoters or PE exits without a clear business reinvestment thesis.

## SME IPOs
SME IPOs list on the BSE SME or NSE Emerge platforms with a minimum lot size designed for sophisticated investors (typically ₹1.0–₹1.4 lakh per lot). They carry materially higher liquidity and governance risk than Mainboard issues. SMIFS engages with SME IPOs only on a case-by-case basis where the issuer demonstrates audited profitability for three years, an experienced board, and a credible growth narrative.

## Anchor Investor Allocation
Anchor investors are QIBs that commit ahead of the public offer (one working day before issue opens) and are subject to a 30-day lock-in. The composition of anchor book is one of our strongest signals — issues with high-quality domestic mutual fund and insurance anchors typically demonstrate stronger post-listing performance.

## Application Mechanism
All retail and HNI applications are processed via UPI (for amounts ≤ ₹5 lakh) or ASBA (Application Supported by Blocked Amount) for higher tickets. Funds are blocked in the applicant's bank account and debited only on allotment. SMIFS clients receive a curated short-list of upcoming issues with our research view, recommended subscription category, and expected listing-day strategy.

## Listing-Day Strategy
SMIFS distinguishes between flipping candidates (issues priced for listing pop) and long-term portfolio additions. For flipping candidates, we recommend exit at listing if the issue lists at or above 15% premium. For portfolio additions, we provide an entry zone and risk stops based on anchor lock-in expiry windows.

## Compliance
All applications adhere to SEBI's UPI mandate framework and the operational framework circular dated November 2018 governing book-built public issues.
