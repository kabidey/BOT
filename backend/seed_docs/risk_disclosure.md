# Risk Disclosure Statement

## General Investment Risk
Investments in securities markets are subject to market risks. The value of a portfolio can rise or fall depending on factors and forces affecting the capital markets. Past performance does not guarantee or indicate future returns. Neither **Mackertich ONE** nor its parent **SMIFS Ltd** makes any representation or warranty regarding the future performance of any security, fund, or strategy recommended.

## Equity-Specific Risks
- **Market Risk**: Equity prices fluctuate with broad market sentiment, interest rates, currency movements, and macroeconomic conditions.
- **Concentration Risk**: Portfolios with high allocation to a single sector, theme, or stock face amplified drawdowns during adverse conditions.
- **Liquidity Risk**: Mid- and small-cap stocks may experience widening bid-ask spreads and execution slippage during market stress.
- **Currency Risk**: International equity exposure introduces INR-USD (or other) movement risk on top of underlying performance.

## Debt-Specific Risks
- **Credit Risk**: The issuer may default on coupon or principal repayment. Lower-rated paper carries higher credit risk.
- **Interest Rate Risk**: Bond prices move inversely to yields. Long-duration paper experiences larger price moves for a given yield change.
- **Reinvestment Risk**: Coupons received during the holding period may have to be reinvested at lower prevailing yields.
- **Liquidity Risk**: Many corporate bonds and NCDs trade thinly in secondary markets; exit before maturity may be at a discount.

## Alternative Investment Risks
AIFs and PMS strategies carry additional risks beyond conventional vehicles, including:

- **Lock-in and Illiquidity**: Most Category I and II AIFs are close-ended with multi-year lock-ins. Capital cannot be redeemed until distribution events.
- **Concentration**: PMS portfolios are typically concentrated in 15–30 securities to drive alpha; this also amplifies single-name risk.
- **Strategy Risk**: Category III AIFs employ leverage and derivatives; losses can exceed initial returns and approach (but cannot exceed) the original investment.

## Tax Law Risk
Indian tax law affecting capital markets is subject to periodic amendment. Material recent changes include the April 2023 reclassification of debt mutual fund taxation, the 2024 LTCG rate revision on equity, and ongoing dialogue around AIF and PMS pass-through treatment. SMIFS provides tax-aware advice but does not act as a tax advisor; clients should consult a qualified chartered accountant for personal tax planning.

## Operational and Custodial Risks
While client assets are held in segregated demat accounts, operational risks at the depository, RTA, or custodian level can lead to delays in corporate action processing, dividend credits, or statement availability. SMIFS does not custody client assets or have signing authority on client bank accounts.

## Behavioural Risk
The single greatest risk to long-term wealth creation in our experience is behavioural — selling during drawdowns, chasing performance, and abandoning a sound asset allocation in response to news flow. Our advisory process is structured around a written IPS specifically to reduce the impact of behavioural reactions on the investor's actual return outcomes.
