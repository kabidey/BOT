# KYC, Compliance, and Onboarding

## Overview
SMIFS Management Services Limited adheres to all client onboarding requirements stipulated by SEBI, the Reserve Bank of India, and the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002. Every relationship begins with a comprehensive Know Your Customer (KYC) process which is a one-time, market-wide registration honoured across all SEBI-registered intermediaries.

## Mandatory KYC Documents
For Indian resident individuals:

- **Identity Proof**: PAN Card (mandatory, non-substitutable).
- **Address Proof**: Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Passport, or Driving Licence. The current address may differ from the document; in that case a recent utility bill or bank statement (not older than 90 days) is also required.
- **Photograph**: Recent passport-size photograph for physical KYC; selfie for digital KYC (CKYC).
- **Bank Proof**: Cancelled cheque or recent bank statement evidencing account number and IFSC.
- **Income Proof**: Salary slip, Form 16, or ITR for the most recent two assessment years (required for HNI and PMS onboarding).

For NRI clients, additional documents include passport with valid visa, OCI/PIO card if applicable, NRE/NRO bank account proof, and FATCA self-declaration.

## CKYC and KRA
Once submitted, KYC is registered with one of SEBI's KYC Registration Agencies (KRAs) — CVL, NDML, Karvy, CAMS, or NSE — and a CKYC Identification Number (CKYC ID) is issued. This 14-digit number can be quoted across financial intermediaries to skip repeat KYC. SMIFS clients can complete the entire process digitally via Aadhaar-based eKYC in under 10 minutes.

## FATCA and CRS Declarations
Under the Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (FATCA, US) and the Common Reporting Standard (CRS, OECD), every Indian financial intermediary is required to collect tax residency self-declarations from each client. The declaration is mandatory at onboarding and must be updated upon any change in tax residency.

## Risk Profiling
Before any product recommendation, SMIFS conducts a structured risk-profiling assessment covering investment objectives, time horizon, liquidity needs, prior investment experience, and behavioural reaction to drawdowns. The output is a documented Investor Risk Profile (IRP) — Conservative, Moderate, Balanced, Growth, or Aggressive — which governs the universe of suitable products. Client recommendations outside the IRP require an explicit risk-acknowledgement from the client.

## SEBI Compliance
SMIFS operates as a SEBI-registered intermediary and adheres to the SEBI (Investor Protection and Education) Regulations, the Disclosure Standards, and quarterly reporting under the relevant compliance framework. All client communication, including this advisory chat, is preserved as part of our audit trail in accordance with applicable record-retention norms.

## Anti-Money Laundering
All transactions above ₹10 lakh in cash or ₹50 lakh aggregate per quarter trigger enhanced due diligence. SMIFS does not accept cash investments under any circumstances; all client funds flow through banking channels traceable to a KYC-verified bank account.

## Grievance Redressal
Clients may raise concerns via our Investor Grievance email or escalate to the SEBI SCORES portal at www.scores.gov.in.
