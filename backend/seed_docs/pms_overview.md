# Portfolio Management Services (PMS)

## Overview
Portfolio Management Services (PMS) are professional investment management offerings registered with SEBI under the SEBI (Portfolio Managers) Regulations, 2020. A PMS provider manages a tailored portfolio of equity, debt, or hybrid securities on behalf of an individual investor in a segregated demat account. Unlike mutual funds, the investor remains the direct legal owner of every underlying security. **Mackertich ONE**, the wealth-management vertical of SMIFS Ltd, partners with select PMS managers across thematic, multi-cap, mid-cap, and concentrated portfolios.

## Discretionary vs Non-Discretionary
SEBI permits two PMS structures:

- **Discretionary PMS**: The portfolio manager has full authority to make buy and sell decisions on behalf of the client within the agreed investment mandate. The client receives quarterly statements and an audited annual report. This is the dominant model in India and accounts for over 95% of PMS AUM.
- **Non-Discretionary PMS**: The portfolio manager advises and executes only with prior client approval for each trade. Suitable for clients who wish to retain decision authority but require institutional research and execution capabilities.

A third historical category, advisory PMS, has been largely subsumed by Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) regulations.

## Minimum Ticket Size
The SEBI-mandated minimum is **₹50 lakh** per investor — raised from ₹25 lakh in 2020 to ensure PMS remains targeted at sophisticated investors. SMIFS recommends PMS only when the client's equity allocation comfortably exceeds ₹2 crore, ensuring adequate diversification across at least three managers and styles.

## Fee Structures
PMS managers typically charge one of three fee combinations:

- **Fixed fee only**: 2.0%–2.5% of AUM annually, billed quarterly. Predictable but does not align manager-client interests on outperformance.
- **Performance fee only (with hurdle)**: 20% of returns above a hurdle (commonly 10% or benchmark). Strong alignment but unpredictable cost.
- **Hybrid**: 1.0%–1.5% fixed plus 10%–15% performance above hurdle. The most common structure for established managers.

GST at 18% applies to all PMS fees. The high-water mark principle ensures performance fees are not paid twice on the same gains.

## Taxation
Since the investor directly owns the securities, taxation flows through transparently:

- **Short-term capital gains** (equity held < 12 months): 15% (plus surcharge and cess).
- **Long-term capital gains** (equity held > 12 months): 10% on gains above ₹1 lakh per financial year.
- **Dividends**: Taxed at slab rate in the investor's hands.

The investor receives a detailed capital gains statement at year-end which is filed in the ITR. This direct-ownership transparency is a key differentiator over mutual funds.

## SMIFS Selection Framework
We evaluate PMS managers across five parameters: investment philosophy clarity, style consistency through cycles, portfolio concentration discipline, risk-adjusted track record over 5+ years, and the personal capital commitment of the principal. We do not maintain a fee-based product distribution model; our PMS recommendations reflect manager merit rather than commercial incentives.
