# Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs)

## Overview
Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) are privately-pooled investment vehicles registered with SEBI under the SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012. AIFs collect capital from sophisticated investors and deploy it into asset classes that fall outside the scope of conventional mutual funds — private equity, venture capital, structured credit, distressed assets, real estate, hedge strategies, and listed market opportunities. SMIFS Management Services Limited curates AIF allocations for high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth clients seeking differentiated risk-return profiles.

## Categories
SEBI classifies AIFs into three categories, each with distinct mandates and regulatory treatment:

### Category I AIF
Funds that invest in start-ups, early-stage ventures, social ventures, SMEs, infrastructure, and other sectors which the government considers economically and socially desirable. Sub-categories include Venture Capital Funds, SME Funds, Social Venture Funds, and Infrastructure Funds. Cat I AIFs typically enjoy pass-through tax status, meaning income is taxed in the hands of the investor rather than the fund.

### Category II AIF
The most common category for HNI portfolios. Includes private equity funds, debt funds, real estate funds, and funds of funds that do not undertake leverage other than to meet day-to-day operational requirements. Cat II covers the broadest spectrum of strategies and is the workhorse of Indian alternatives. Tax treatment: pass-through for capital gains and dividends, fund-level taxation for business income.

### Category III AIF
Funds that employ diverse or complex trading strategies including leverage, derivatives, and short-selling. Hedge funds and long-short equity strategies sit here. Cat III funds are taxed at the fund level on all income — investors receive net distributions.

## Minimum Ticket Size
SEBI mandates a minimum investment of ₹1 crore per investor in any AIF, irrespective of category. The minimum corpus of an AIF scheme is ₹20 crore. SMIFS evaluates AIF allocations only for clients whose total investable surplus comfortably accommodates a ₹1 crore commitment without compromising liquidity for goals within a five-year horizon.

## Lock-in and Tenure
Most Category I and II AIFs are close-ended with a tenure of 5–10 years and a hard lock-in. Capital is drawn down progressively over a 2–4 year investment period and returned over a similar harvest period. Category III AIFs are typically open-ended with monthly or quarterly redemption windows, though some offer initial lock-ins of 6–12 months.

## Investor Eligibility
AIF units may be subscribed only by accredited investors meeting one of: (a) HNI status with the ₹1 crore minimum, (b) institutional investors, or (c) NRI/foreign investors via the FPI route. SMIFS conducts a structured suitability assessment before recommending any AIF, covering investor net worth, liquidity needs, time horizon, and prior alternatives experience.

## Reporting
AIFs file quarterly reports with SEBI and provide audited annual financials and unit NAV statements to investors. Trustees independently oversee compliance with the placement memorandum.
